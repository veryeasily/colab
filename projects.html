Each year, we hold an event on the land to share knowledge, project ideas, labor, and discourse. While many active members attend, we use it as a time to network and share with others who have similar interests and incorporate new people into looking to be more involved. The social part of the conference usually takes place over a holiday weekend and an extended time for project work usually takes place the week before or after.

Quantified Self tumblr Fall 2015 - Spring 2016
Three CoLab members, Jackie Cameron, Micha Gorelick, and Michael Skirpan, co-produced an immersive theater piece about social issues involving data and technology. Produced with the help of University of Colorado, Fast Forward Labs, and the John S. and James L. Knight Foundation, the production used audience member data to personalize 10 interactive art installations inside the set as a sci-fi drama happened around them.

CoLab played an important role in the initial brainstorming, script writing process, and final design and set build.

Worped Salon Facebook Spring 2016 - Present
Worped is a repeating salon for performing arts that occurs on a near-monthly basis. The project was initated to give artists a chance to community-build and collaborate, as well as to test new ideas. Feedback is considered an important part of the creative process, and in some sense, an art in-itself. Accordingly each night ends in a workshop that provides a central means of refining pieces and encouraging interdependence.

Grundy Quarrels Writers' Retreat Fall 2015 - Present
Colab contributors have set up a yearly writers' retreat down on the land. The land provides a reliable space to retreat into one's work but still having a backdrop of community that encourages artistic risk tasking.
